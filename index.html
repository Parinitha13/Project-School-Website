    Blue Ribbon School

    High School Ranking

    Churchill Junior High School is ranked within the top 20% of all 2,221 schools in New Jersey (based off of combined math and reading proficiency testing data) for the 2018-2019 school year. The percentage of students achieving proficiency in math is 62% (which is higher than the New Jersey state average of 44%) for the 2018-19 school year. The percentage of students achieving proficiency in reading/language arts is 77% (which is higher than the New Jersey state average of 57%) for the 2018-19 school year. Thus, Churchill Junior High School is considered the best junior high school of the East Brunswick District in New Jersey with a rating of 9/10 qualifying it as one of the 11 blue ribbon schools of East Brunswick. Churchill Junior High School promotes superior standards of academic excellence amongst students and it is one of the many blue ribbon schools in New Jersey State to have the highest overall test ranking proficiency of math and reading.



    Counseling Department

    Guidance Counseling

    The Counseling Department of Churchill Junior High School intends to endorse and influence academic and social/emotional development of students. Counselors aim to assist and support students' whenever they are in trouble or suffering from mental problems like stress, depression or anxiety. Students can book an appointment with their counselor anytime throughout their school day and can share personal feelings, thoughts or their preferences on electives and request for change in classes. The program is beneficial in students' acknowledgement of their full academic potential, optimal personal growth, and acquire optimistic approaches, social skills.



    Community Service

    Community Service

    The Churchill Junior High School Community Service Program fosters the development of democratic values, compassion, willingness, and benevolent nature of protecting the environment and to develop a deep respect, admiration towards taking care of Mother Earth.     What's New

  • Change in Bell Schedule

    Block Scheduling has been implemented this year in Churchill Junior High School, class periods are split into two days, A-Days where periods 1-4 are taught, conducted, and the next school day, B-day where students attend periods 5-8. Additionally, there are different lunch periods like 3A/7A lunch, 3B/7B lunch, and 3C/7C lunch, amd lunch periods differ for both A-Days and B-days. Block scheduling lengthened class time, classes that were usually 40 minutes in length are now doubled to 82 minutes per period. Lunch period is also changed from 20 minutes to 39 minutes.



  • Change in Accomodation of Grade Levels

    East Brunsiwck Board of Education aimed to reconfigue grade levels of the East Brunswick Public Schools and add additonal classrooms to the temporarily existing 30 classrooms of Churchill Junior High School. Initially, the eight elementary schools of the East Brunswick District housed students from grades K-4, Hammarskjold Middle School housed grades 5-6, and Churchill Junior High School housed students from grades 7-9, and East Brunswick High School housed students from grades 10-12. The reconfiguration measure was implemented and the changes were that Elementary Schools in the East Brunsiwck district housed students in grade levels K-5, Hammarskjold Middle School has grades 6-7, Churchill accomodated rades 8-9, and East Brunswick High School continues to house students from grades 10-12. The renovation of the number of classrooms in Churchill Junior High School of 32 temporary classrooms were identical to exiting junior high school classrooms in terms of furnishing, technology, and climate management. The plan was in response to a projected increase in the number of students over the next three years. The idea had three distinct priorities- beneficial in developing a sustainable elementary capacity in all elementary schools of the East Brunswick District and focuses on equity across elementary schools to maintain costs and minimize tax impacts on the residents of the East Brunswick Community.

    First Announcement

    Science Olympiad and Curriculum

    The 8th grade Science Curriculum at Churchill Junior High School introduces students to chemistry, geology, meteorology, and astronomy. Students will conduct several laboratory experiments in their science classes. There are two levels, 8th Grade Academic Science and 8th Grade Honors Science. The 9th grade Science Curriculum offers three levels of biology that consists of topics like structure and function of matter and organisms, inheritance and variation of traits, natural selection and evolution, and human impact on populations and communities. The three levels of 9th Grade Biology are Biology Honors, Biology Accelerated, and Biology. Extra-curricular activities and Science Clubs include The Science Olympiad and Science League. The Science Olympiad team competes in a regional competition in January, then top teams advance to the state competition in March. Students try out for a position on the team during the first marking period of the school year, and meetings are held twice each week. Advisors to the CJHS team are William Jasko and Rebecca Cobb. Students participating in Science League take a monthly biology test at EBHS after school from January-April. Students try out for a position on the team during the first semester, and weekly meetings are held. The advisor to the team is Maria Gorman.

    Third Announcement

    Choir Concerts

    Choir provides students with an opportunity to sing in a choral setting during the school day. Students will learn about different styles of choral music through performance. Students will also learn aspects of music history, sight-singing, dynamics, solfage, and music theory. Chorus Concerts account for 60% of overall grade, participation and contribution accounts 20%, and assessments are 20% of the final grade. The CJHS Musical Winter Concerts occur in the month of December. CJHS Orchestra Concert took place on December 6th, 2021, CJHS Band Concert happened on December 15th, 2021, and the CJHS Vocal Choir Concert transpired on December 20th, 2021. Chorus Winter Concert Attire must be all black, black from top to bottom and they will report to the cafeteria by 6:25pm for check-in atttendance. The Concert will take place at 7pm and Chorus students will perform 5 pieces in different languages in the order, Walk Through Life(English upbeat song), Durme Durme(Spanish-Latin Sephardic Lullaby), Kuwa Furaha(Swahili Joyous song), O Magnum Mysterium(Latin Song), and Sleigh Bells(Russian Folk Song).
